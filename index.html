About

Science, technology,
and community.

I am a geologist from northern Nigeria with a growing interest in how science and technology can expand opportunity in places often overlooked.

I graduated as the overall best student with distinction from Abubakar Tatari Ali Polytechnic in Bauchi, where I developed a deep interest in applying data and geospatial thinking beyond traditional academic fields.

I have participated in the UNDP Digital for Youth Led Enterprise programme and the national 3 Million Technical Talent initiative, exploring artificial intelligence and machine learning. Currently I am a fellow in a renewable energy leadership programme, learning about distributed energy systems.

My long-term interest lies in combining science, technology, and local knowledge to develop ideas that contribute to more inclusive development.

Field Stories

From the ground

Bauchi State · Heritage

Visiting the Tomb of Tafawa Balewa

During a visit to Bauchi State, I documented the historic site of Nigeria's first Prime Minister. Standing at the site reminded me how landscapes often hold layers of historical memory — places like this connect national history with local geography.

Bauchi State · Geography

Communities Living on Rock Formations

In parts of Bauchi State, families have lived for generations on large rock formations. Documenting these places revealed how people adapt to their environment and how geography shapes daily life in ways often overlooked in urban narratives.

Northern Nigeria · Energy

Observing Energy Access in Rural Areas

Through my renewable energy fellowship and field observations, I have become increasingly interested in the role of decentralized energy systems. Access to reliable energy remains one of the most important foundations for education, healthcare, and economic activity.
