About

Science, community,
and what gets left out.

I am a 24-year-old geologist from northern Nigeria with a distinction in Geology from Abubakar Tatari Ali Polytechnic, Bauchi — where I graduated as the overall best student. My work sits at the intersection of geoscience, digital innovation, and community documentation.

I co-founded Tinder Health, a digital healthcare navigation platform that has mapped 125+ facilities across the Suleja–Abuja corridor — addressing the gap between patients and the right care. Our research revealed that 92.9% of referrals fail due to navigation gaps.

Through Moh Shotss, I spend time documenting landscapes, heritage spaces, and communities across northern Nigeria — particularly communities that exist outside every formal record. This work led to KundiMap, a community-driven documentation framework designed to connect invisible communities to the systems that should protect them.

Currently I am a NextGen RESCO Intern at Ashipa Electric through the Rural Electrification Agency, working on renewable energy deployment in underserved communities. I was selected for the African Impact Challenge 2026 and recognised as First Runner-Up at the UNDP Digital for Youth-Led Enterprise programme.

01 / Documentation Framework

KundiMap

A community-powered digital platform and policy framework designed to document, verify, and integrate living heritage communities into formal recognition systems. KundiMap transforms communities that do not exist on any map into digitally documented, policy-recognised, and economically included systems — starting with the rock-dwelling communities of Bauchi metropolis.

Field Stories

From the ground

Bauchi State · Heritage Documentation

Communities Living Between Boulders

The granite inselbergs of Bauchi metropolis stretch across hundreds of metres of urban landscape. Families have built homes between boulders, carved pathways into rock faces, and shaped their lives around a geological landscape of extraordinary cultural significance — for over thirty years, outside every formal record. This is where KundiMap began.

Bauchi State · National Heritage

The Tomb of Nigeria's First Prime Minister

Documenting the historic tomb of Sir Abubakar Tafawa Balewa in Bauchi — declared a national monument in 1979 — revealed the distance between formal recognition and public knowledge. The site exists in the registry. Its story does not exist in the minds of most young Nigerians. Documentation and recognition are not the same thing.

Northern Nigeria · Renewable Energy

Energy Access and the Communities Without It

Through the NextGen RESCO internship at Ashipa Electric and the Rural Electrification Agency, I have been working at the intersection of distributed energy systems and the communities they are meant to serve. Reliable energy is not a luxury — it is the infrastructure on which education, healthcare, and economic agency are built.
